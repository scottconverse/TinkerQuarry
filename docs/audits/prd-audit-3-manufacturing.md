**Date:** 2026-06-21 ¬∑ **Auditor scope:** Printability gate, Orient, Slice, Send + real-hardware, and their screens.
**Method:** Read the shipping SPA (`KimCadClaude/frontend/src`), the engine (`KimCadClaude/src/kimcad`), `docs/api.md`, and the glue backend (`tinkerquarry/backend`). Citations are `file:line`.

**Headline:** The engine side of this pipeline is genuinely strong and mostly PRESENT ‚Äî the gate, orient math, slicer with G-code proof, six real connectors, server-side gate re-checks, and an honest simulated-vs-real send distinction are all real and well-tested. The gaps are almost entirely on the **UI surface** and in **two named typed-states**: (1) there is **no manual-orient override** anywhere ‚Äî orientation is auto-only with a static label; (2) the **"first-real-send" extra-caution maturity state does not exist** in any layer; and (3) **profiles are not shown in plain language *before* slicing** (only printer + material dropdowns; the full profile line is returned *after* the slice). The PRD's "6 distinct send maturity states" are partially collapsed into a binary simulated/real split.

## 5-line summary
1. **Engine: strong and PRESENT.** Gate (watertight/wall/volume/shells), auto-orient, OrcaSlicer with a real motion-bearing G-code *proof*, six LAN connectors, exact `confirm=True` enforcement, and server-side gate re-checks on both slice and send are real, fail-closed, and well-commented.
2. **Three hard gaps, all UI/state-level:** **manual-orient override is entirely MISSING** (auto-only, static "plate-down" chip not even bound to the actual pose); **first-real-send extra-caution maturity state is entirely MISSING** (1st physical print confirms identically to the 100th); and **profiles are not shown in plain language before slicing** (no "Bambu P1S ¬∑ PLA ¬∑ 0.2mm" line, layer height shown nowhere).
3. **Partial gaps:** one-step fix paths are descriptive prose, not actionable buttons; the `stale` can't-slice reason is never emitted (2 of 3); printer *capabilities* (build volume/nozzle/materials) are modeled but not surfaced on the connection UI; printing-progress shows binary state, not the engine's available percentage.
4. **Done well and honestly:** the simulated-vs-real distinction is rigorous (never narrates a mock as a real print), per-send confirmation is airtight, and the post-print outcome flow is correctly real-only and local-only.
5. **Hand-off read:** this is roughly a fortnight of *front-end + glue* work (orient override endpoint + UI, first-real-send state, pre-slice profile line, capability display, `stale` detection), not a rebuild ‚Äî the load-bearing manufacturing engine is the part you'd most want to keep.
